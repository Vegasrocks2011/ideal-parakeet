Which structure controls what enters and leaves the cell?

BCell membrane
CNucleus
DVacuole

The cell membrane is selectively permeable — it controls which substances move in and out of the cell.

Which of the following is found in plant cells but NOT in animal cells?
ANucleus
BCell membrane
CChloroplast
DMitochondria
✓ Answer: C — Chloroplast
Chloroplasts are only found in plant cells. They contain chlorophyll and are the site of photosynthesis.
📖 Revise: differences between plant and animal cells

AAnimal cell
BBacterial cell

DFungal cell

Plant cells have all three: cellulose cell walls, a large central vacuole, and chloroplasts.

Foundation

AOrgan → cell → tissue → organism
BCell → tissue → organ → organism

DCell → organ → tissue → organism

The hierarchy is: cell → tissue → organ → organ system → organism.
📖 Revise: levels of organisation in multicellular organisms

AActive movement against a concentration gradient
BMovement of particles from low to high concentration
CMovement of particles from high to low concentration

Diffusion is passive — no energy required. Particles move down their concentration gradient from high to low.

Gas exchange
4 questions · BIO-Y7-GAS
BIO-016
Foundation
What happens to the diaphragm when we breathe in?
AIt relaxes and moves up
BIt contracts and moves down
CIt stays in the same position
DIt moves sideways
✓ Answer: B
When we inhale the diaphragm contracts and flattens (moves down), increasing chest volume and drawing air in.
📖 Revise: the mechanism of breathing — diaphragm and intercostal muscles
BIO-017
Standard
Which gas moves from the blood into the alveoli to be breathed out?
AOxygen
BNitrogen
CCarbon dioxide
DWater vapour
✓ Answer: C — Carbon dioxide
CO₂ is a waste product of respiration. It diffuses from the blood (high concentration) into the alveoli (low concentration) and is breathed out.
📖 Revise: gas exchange at the alveoli — what moves in and out
BIO-018
Standard
Which feature of alveoli makes them well adapted for gas exchange?
AThick walls
BLarge volume
CLarge surface area and moist lining
DSurrounded by cartilage
✓ Answer: C
Alveoli have a very large total surface area, thin walls (one cell thick), a moist lining, and a rich blood supply — all adaptations for efficient gas exchange.
📖 Revise: adaptations of alveoli for efficient gas exchange
BIO-019
Standard
In plants, which structures allow gas exchange through the leaf?
AChloroplasts
BStomata
CXylem
DRoot hairs
✓ Answer: B — Stomata
Stomata are tiny pores mainly on the underside of leaves. They open and close to allow CO₂ in for photosynthesis and O₂ out.
📖 Revise: stomata and gas exchange in plants

AGrow faster
BProduce more oxygen

BIO-032
Foundation
In a food web, what does an arrow represent?
AWhere the organism lives
BThe flow of energy from prey to predator
CCompetition between species
DThe size of the population
✓ Answer: B
Arrows show the flow of energy from the organism being eaten to the organism eating it — they point in the direction energy travels.
📖 Revise: reading and interpreting food webs — what arrows mean
BIO-033
Standard
If the rabbit population suddenly decreased, what would most likely happen to the fox population?
AIncrease
BStay the same
CDecrease
DNot be affected
✓ Answer: C — Decrease
Foxes depend on rabbits as a food source. If rabbits decline, foxes have less food and their population falls too — this shows predator-prey interdependence.
📖 Revise: predator-prey relationships and interdependence in ecosystems
BIO-034
Standard
Which term describes all populations of different species living in the same area?
AHabitat
BEcosystem
CCommunity
DPopulation
✓ Answer: C — Community
A community is all populations of different species in the same area. An ecosystem includes the community plus the non-living (abiotic) environment.
📖 Revise: key ecology terms — population, community, ecosystem, habitat
BIO-035
Standard
Why is biodiversity important?
AIt makes ecosystems look attractive
BIt ensures ecosystems are more stable and resilient to change
CIt increases the number of predators only
DIt reduces competition between species
✓ Answer: B
High biodiversity makes ecosystems more stable. If one species is lost, others can fill its role. Low biodiversity makes ecosystems fragile and vulnerable.
📖 Revise: the importance of biodiversity and ecosystem stability
BIO-036
Foundation
Which of the following is an example of a producer in an ecosystem?
AFox
BRabbit
CGrass
DHawk
✓ Answer: C — Grass
Producers make their own food via photosynthesis. Grass is a producer. Foxes, rabbits and hawks are all consumers.
📖 Revise: producers and consumers in food chains

BAbility to roll your tongue

Continuous variation shows a range of values with no distinct categories — height can be any value across a spectrum. The others are discontinuous (distinct categories).

BIO-040
Standard
Natural selection occurs because:
AAll individuals in a species are identical
BSome individuals have variations that make them better adapted to survive and reproduce
CThe environment never changes
DOrganisms choose to evolve
✓ Answer: B
Individuals with advantageous variations are more likely to survive, reproduce, and pass on those traits. Over generations this leads to evolution.

BEnvironmental change that reduces the species' ability to compete and reproduce

DStable environment

Extinction occurs when environmental change means individuals of a species can no longer compete successfully and reproduce — the population drops to zero.
